Irene Dunne was one of the most versatile actresses of Hollywood's Golden Era. She did it all; cronicle drama (Cimarron), musical comedy (Show Boat, Roberta), soap opera (Back Street, Magnificent Obsession), screwball comedy (The Awful Truth, My Favorite Wife), romance (Love Affair), tearjerker (Penny Serenade) and period drama (Anna and the King of Siam, I Remember Mama). She recieved 5 best actress Oscar nominations along the way, and left the screen while still in demand. A class act, all the way.
