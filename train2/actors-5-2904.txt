Paul Newman himself might see himself as a less-than-fantastic actor, but the rest of us know better. Aside from the fact that he was quite brilliant in the movie version of Tennesse Williams' classic play, Cat On A Hot Tin Roof, Newman is one of the few remaining old Real Great Moviestars from an era when stars like Elizabeth Taylor, James Dean and Marilyn Monroe were around. Sure, he's not THE finest actor ever, not at all. But you gotta hand it to him - he's darn handsome, and he's got that certain quality I believe begins with star ...
