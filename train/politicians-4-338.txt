George was largely passive as King, preferring to spend most of his time away from the throne. George's apathy was surprisingly effective at maintaining political and social tranquility in Britain. He relied heavily on subordinates to represent the monarchy, thus leading to the creation of the office of the Prime Minister, a major step in the formation of the modern English government. In foreign affairs, George's administration was successful in avoiding war with foreign powers and forging peaceful alliances. Meanwhile, the English colonies in America were thriving. Great Britain was the most powerful nation in the world by this time. Overall, George was effective at doing nothing, which was just what Britain needed to preserve tranquility and prosperity.
