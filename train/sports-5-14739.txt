He is the best quarterback of the modern era with the exception of Farve (still writing his own books). He took his team to 3 super bowls with NO offense. He is the most sacked quarterback of all time and yet he is also the most durable (6 games missed). If you factor in the fact that he was one of the best scramblers that says a lot about how crappy his O-line was in the early years. If you take Marino or Montana and put them in the early system they wouldn't have lasted 5 years. Put Elway in those systems and he might have broken all the records. Had all the tools - strong arm, quick release, scrambling and rushing ability, and the ability to shrug off bad plays. Most of all, he was a winner.
